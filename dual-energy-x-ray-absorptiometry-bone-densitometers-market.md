<p><p>Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (DXA) Bone Densitometers are used to measure bone density and assess the risk of fractures due to conditions such as osteoporosis. There are two main types of DXA bone densitometers: Axial Bone Densitometers, which scan the spine and hip to measure bone density in these central areas, and Peripheral Bone Densitometers, which assess bone density in peripheral areas such as the wrist, heel, or finger. Both types of bone densitometers play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring bone health.</p></p>
