<p><p>Solid State Oxygen Fuel Cells are classified into two main types based on their design: Planar and Tubular. Planar solid state fuel cells have a flat design that allows for a high power density and compact size, making them ideal for small-scale applications. On the other hand, Tubular solid state fuel cells have a tubular shape that provides a larger surface area for fuel and oxygen reaction, resulting in higher efficiency and scalability for larger power applications. Each type offers distinct advantages based on the specific requirements of the fuel cell system.</p></p>
