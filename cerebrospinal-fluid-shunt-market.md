<p><p>Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) shunts are used to treat conditions such as hydrocephalus by diverting excess fluid to another part of the body. The main types of CSF shunts include Ventriculoperitoneal (VP), Ventriculoatrial (VA), Lumboperitoneal (LP), and Ventriculopleural (VPL) shunts. VP shunts drain fluid from the ventricles in the brain to the peritoneal cavity, VA shunts drain to the atria of the heart, LP shunts drain to the peritoneal cavity via the lumbar region, and VPL shunts drain to the pleural cavity.</p></p>
