<p><p>Crystalline fructose is a sweetener commonly used in the food industry. It can be produced through two main processes: starch hydrolysis and sucrose hydrolysis. In starch hydrolysis, enzymes break down starch into glucose, which is then converted into fructose through a series of chemical reactions. In sucrose hydrolysis, sucrose is broken down into glucose and fructose using enzymes. Both types of crystalline fructose are widely used in a variety of food products due to their high sweetness and solubility.</p></p>
