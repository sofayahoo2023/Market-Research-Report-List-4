<p><ul><li>Natural Bread Preservative</li><li>Chemical Bread Preservative</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bread preservatives can be categorized into two main types: natural and chemical. Natural bread preservatives are derived from natural sources such as salt, vinegar, and sugar, and are used to extend the shelf life of bread without any synthetic additives. On the other hand, chemical bread preservatives are made from synthetic compounds such as calcium propionate and potassium sorbate, which are added to bread to inhibit the growth of mold and bacteria. Both types of preservatives serve the purpose of increasing the longevity of bread products.</p></p>
