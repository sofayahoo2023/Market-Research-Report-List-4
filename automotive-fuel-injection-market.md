<p><p>Automotive fuel injection systems are classified into three main types: Port Injection, Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI), and Diesel Direct Injection. </p><p>- Port Injection: In this system, fuel is injected into the intake port of the engine before it reaches the combustion chamber. It is often seen in older vehicles.</p><p>- Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI): GDI systems inject fuel directly into the combustion chamber, resulting in more efficient fuel delivery and improved engine performance.</p><p>- Diesel Direct Injection: In this system, fuel is directly injected into the combustion chamber of a diesel engine for better fuel economy and reduced emissions.</p></p>
