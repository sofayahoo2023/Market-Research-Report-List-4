<p><ul><li>Split-thickness</li><li>Full-thickness</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Frozen skin grafts are classified into two types based on the thickness of the skin removed from the donor site: split-thickness and full-thickness. Split-thickness skin grafts involve taking a thin layer of skin, including the epidermis and part of the dermis, while full-thickness skin grafts include the entire epidermis and dermis. Split-thickness grafts are commonly used for larger wounds that need to heal quickly, while full-thickness grafts are used for smaller wounds or areas where a more natural appearance is desired.</p></p>
