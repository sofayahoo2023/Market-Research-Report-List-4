<p><p>Photo-electric sensors are commonly categorized into four main types based on their operating principles. Through-beam sensors consist of separate emitter and receiver units placed opposite to each other. Retro-reflective sensors use a reflector to bounce light back to the sensor. Diffuse-reflective sensors detect light reflected directly off the object being sensed. Other types of photo-electric sensors include polarized sensors, background suppression sensors, and fiber optic sensors, each designed for specific applications and environments.</p></p>
