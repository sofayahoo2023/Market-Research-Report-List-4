<p><p>Gestational surrogacy involves the implantation of an embryo created through in vitro fertilization into the surrogate's uterus. This type of surrogacy is preferred as it eliminates the genetic connection between the surrogate and the child. On the other hand, traditional surrogacy involves the surrogate using her own egg to conceive the child, making her the biological mother. Both types of surrogacy provide options for individuals or couples who are unable to conceive on their own.</p></p>
