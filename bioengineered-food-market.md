<p><p>Bioengineered food, also known as genetically modified (GM) food, refers to food products that have been genetically altered through biotechnology techniques. Bioengineered food has become increasingly popular in recent years due to its potential to address food security issues, improve crop yields, and reduce the use of chemical pesticides.</p><p>The global bioengineered food market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.8% during the forecast period. Additionally, advancements in biotechnology and genetic engineering techniques are enabling the development of more innovative bioengineered food products.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the bioengineered food market include the commercialization of genetically modified crops with enhanced nutritional value, such as vitamin-enriched rice and protein-fortified maize. Moreover, there is a growing focus on developing bioengineered food products with reduced allergenic properties and improved taste profiles. Overall, the bioengineered food market is expected to continue expanding as consumers increasingly seek out healthier and more sustainable food choices.</p></p>
