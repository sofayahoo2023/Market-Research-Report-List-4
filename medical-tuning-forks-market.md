<p><p>Medical tuning forks are devices used to assess a patient's hearing and nerve function. There are two main types of tuning forks commonly used in the medical field - Hartmann and Rydel-Seiffer. Hartmann tuning forks are typically utilized for hearing tests and to determine the vibration sensitivity of nerves. On the other hand, Rydel-Seiffer tuning forks are specifically designed to assess the vibratory perception and proprioception of a patient's nerves. Both types play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring various medical conditions.</p></p>
